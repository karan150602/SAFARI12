Chobe National Park

Chobe National Park is in northern Botswana near the vast, inland Okavango Delta. It's known for its large herds of elephants and Cape buffalo, which converge along the Chobe Riverfront in the dry months. Lions, antelopes and hippos inhabit the woods and lagoons around Linyanti Marsh. The floodable grasslands of the Savuti Marsh attract numerous bird species, plus migrating zebras.Chobe National Park is extremely lush because of the many waterways. The gateway to Chobe is Kasane, a small town near the borders of Zimbabwe, Zambia and Namibia. Guests can transfer from Kasane to their respective accommodation in and around Chobe National Park.

Best time to visit

August to October (Best at the riverfront area)

High season

July to October (The park gets crowded)

Best weather

May to August (Less hot during the day)
